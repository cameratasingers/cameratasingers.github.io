Richard D. Holmes
Photos and Paragraphs

Dick's Parents
  
   
Dick's parents are William Harry Holmes and Julia Handzo. They met on a blind date in 1927 and were married in November 1928. Harry left school after the eighth grade to work in a coal mine with his father and brothers. Julia is the daughter of a Slovak immigrant who spent his life as a coal miner, then as a truck farmer. Harry eventually became an automobile mechanic and, shortly after marrying, established his own auto repair/gasoline station in Farmington, IL. Harry was 28 and Julia was 19 in this wedding photo.
  
 
Harry and Julia Holmes, Peoria, IL (1928)
  

 
 
Dick as a Baby
  
   
Born in August 1936 in Peoria, IL, Dick lived in Farmington, a small town in downstate Illinois, until age 18 when he went to college. With a population of 2500, Farmington was dependent upon farming, mining and the Caterpillar Tractor Company in Peoria. Dick's Dad expanded his business to include automobile sales. Julia said that Dick's name resulted from a commitment on Harry's part in order to consummate the sale of a new Hudson to a man named Richard Dean Wiley
  
 
Dick at 8 months, Farmington, IL (1937)
  

 
 
Dick as a Young Boy
  
   
Dick (on the far right) was the third of four boys born to Harry and Julia Holmes. A sister, Barbara Jean, born in 1930, lived only 5 days. In order of age, the sons are Harry, Bob, Dick and Ken. Dick was the first one in the family to be born in a hospital.
  
 
Dick in family photo, Farmington, IL (1942)
  

 
 
Dick in Grade School
  
   
Dick credits much of his interest in school (particularly his interest in geography) to two trips with his parents while still fairly young. The family went west to Yellowstone National Park when Dick was entering the fourth grade and east to Washington, Philadephia and New York when he was entering the fifth grade. Here Dick (with the dark shorts) is with his mother, his older brother, Harry, and younger brother, Ken, in front of the U.S. Capitol building. Dick can recall while travel through a very thick fog in West Virginia that his Dad opened the car door while driving in order to see the center line on the road.
  
 
Dick in Washington, DC (1946)
  

 
 
Dick Graduates from Junior High
  
   
Dick's first music interest was the drums, his Dad's instrument during the 1920's. Dick took private drum lessons when he entered the fourth grade. After two years, he left the drums for something more melodic, and took up the cornet. He played that instrument in various bands for 7 years until graduating from high school. Dick was valedictorian of his relatively small (31) class when he graduated from the eighth grade.
  
 
Dick - Junior High graduation, Farmington, IL (1950)
  

 
 
Dick in High School
  
   
Dick is shown here as a fakir during the freshman initiation ceremonies at Farmington High School. A fairly mediocre athlete, Dick concentrated on music (singing and band) and became the athletic statistician for the school. Dick didn't realize that he couldn't see until he took an eye test for his driver's exam when he was 15. Until then, he thought everyone saw a somewhat "blurry" world.
 
 
Dick as High School Freshman, Farmington, IL (1950)
  

 
 
Dick Graduates from High School
  
   
Dick graduated 3rd in his class (of 75) from Farmington High School. While in school, he worked at his Dad's garage as a bookkeeper, mowed lawns and delivered newspapers. He purchased a soda pop dispensing machine and paid for it over time from the vending machine profits. He recalls the complications associated with raising the price of a soft drink from 5 cents to 6 cents per bottle, requiring a penny hole in the coin slot.
 
 
Dick - High School graduation, Farmington, IL (1954)
  

 
 
Dick at Grinnell College
  
   
Dick entered Grinnell College in Grinnell, IA following graduation from high school. With summer work at Caterpillar, a substantial scholarship and working as a dining room waiter, he was able to personally finance all of his undergraduate education. Shown here as House President for Gates Hall, Dick was very active in extracurricular activities at Grinnell.
  
 
Dick in Gates Hall, Grinnell College, Grinnell, IA (1957)
  

 
 
Dick Graduates from Grinnell College
  
   
Dick graduated from Grinnell in 1958, majoring in both Mathematics and Physics. He was elected to Phi Beta Kappa, was in the Men's Glee Club, the Scarleteers (a Whiffenpoofs-style men's octet), the College Choir, Chief Engineer at KGRW (the college radio station), intramural athletics, was athletic statistician for Grinnell and was elected to two honorary groups -Hermits (Freshman) and Friars (Senior).
 
 
Dick - Grinnell College graduation, Grinnell, IA (1958)
  

 
 
Dick Graduates from M.I.T.
  
   
Dick attended M.I.T. in Cambridge, MA for 3 years, obtaining an M.S. in nuclear physics. While at M.I.T., Dick worked as a teaching assistant, which paid for his education. He sang in the M.I.T. Choral Society and the Logarhythms (another Whiffenpoofs-like male octet). He also served as President of the M.I.T. Graduate House during his final two years. While at M.I.T., Dick won a Boston Globe contest by obtaining more than 3000 different words out of the phrase "Euphrates Pizza" Dick worked at the Lawrence Radiation Lab in Livermore, CA during one of the summers while at M.I.T.
  
 
Dick at MIT with his ukulele and work (??) of art, Cambridge, MA (1960)
  

 
 
Dick in the U.S. Army
  
   
Following graduation from M.I.T., Dick joined Procter & Gamble in Quincy, MA, working as a soap packing line foreman. He progressed to methods engineer and eventually relocated to Cincinnati, OH to work as a computer systems analyst. While at Procter & Gamble in MA, Dick joined the Massachusetts National Guard, serving 6 months active duty (at Fort Dix, NJ) and 5 1/2 years in inactive duty. Dick hated the army.
  
 
Dick in U.S. Army, Fort Dix, NJ (1962)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn Get Married
  
   
While working at Procter & Gamble in Cincinnati, Dick met a cute young school-teacher, Marilyn Grubaugh, who happened to be living in the same apartment building. They were engaged in November 1963 just prior to the Kennedy assassination. They were married in an Episcopal Church in Mansfield, OH in April 1964. Marilyn's father, James Hoffman, offered, as a wedding gift, the opportunity for Dick to attend business school.
 
 
Dick and Marilyn, Mansfield, OH (1964)
  

 
 
Dick Graduates from Stanford University
  
   
Dick selected Stanford University for business school and was accepted for admission in the fall of 1964. He was also accepted at Harvard, but decided to attend Stanford primarily because he had already spent 5 years in Massachusetts. It was a good decision. Dick did well at Stanford, graduating with an M.B.A. in 1966, ranked 7th in a class of over 200 students. He majored in Finance & Operations Research.
  
 
Dick - Stanford Business School graduation, Palo Alto, CA (1966)
  

 
 
Dick Joins Baxter Laboratories
  
   
Following graduation at Stanford, Dick joined Baxter Laboratories in Morton Grove, IL as Assistant to the VP of Administration at Baxter. Dick and Marilyn moved to Deerfield, IL and joined the First Presbyterian Church in Deerfield. He later worked as Product Development Coordinator and then as Assistant to the President. In May 1966, Dick and Marilyn had their first child, James Michael, who was born at the Stanford University Hospital.
  
 
Dick, Marilyn and Mike, Deerfield, IL (1968)
  

 
 
Dick Named Director of R&D for Aminco Division
  
   
In 1970, shortly after the birth of their second son, William Jeffrey, at Highland Park Hospital, Dick was promoted to Director of R & D at Aminco - the instrument division of Baxter. The family moved to Silver Spring, MD and purchased an afghan puppy, Cassandra Kassim of Rock Run.
 
 
Dick with Cassie (2 yrs old), Silver Spring, MD (1973)
  

 
 
Dick Named VP-General Manager Electrotherm
  
   
In 1972, Dick and Marilyn had their third son, Christopher Richard, who was born at Georgetown University Hospital. Marilyn's obstetrician was also Jackie Kennedy's ob/gyn. Dick was promoted to VP-General Manager of Electrotherm - an Aminco subsidiary that specialized in the manufacture of electric heating elements. Later, Dick was promoted to Executive VP for the entire Aminco Division, taking on manufacturing, finance and data processing.
  
 
Dick at Electrotherm, Laurel, MD (1976)
  

 
 
Dick Named President - Baxter Canada
  
   
In 1978, Dick was named President of Baxter Canada and the family relocated to a beautiful Japanese contemporary home in Mississauga, ON. Dick's five years in Canada represent the finest days of his career with Baxter and introduced the family to many wonderful friends who have remained close despite distance and the passage of time. Under Dick's leadership, Baxter Canada was extremely successful and in five years tripled sales to $100 million/year while net profits quintupled.
 
 
Dick at Baxter Canada, Mississauga, ON, Canada (1979)
  

 
 
Dick Named Vice Chairman for PMAC
  
   
One of the perks associated with being President of Canada was being Baxter's representative to the PMAC (Pharm. Mfrs. Assoc. of Canada). This group would meet annually in resort areas (Bermuda for many years, and once in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico). Dick was elected Vice Chairman of the group in 1982 and was slated to be Chairman in 1983
 
 
Dick, Marilyn and Mike and a friend in Bermuda (1982)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go on Sales Incentive Trips
  
   
Probably the most significant perk associated with being President of Baxter Canada was the fringe benefit of attending the sales incentive trips along with the top-achieving salesmen. Trips included Rio de Janeiro; Hawaii (Oahu and Maui); Spain (Madrid and Majorca); Las Vegas; Greece (Athens and Corfu) and Acapulco, Mexico (on the "Love Boat"). This photo was at Las Hadas Manzanillo, the setting for the movie "10" with Bo Derek.
  
 
Dick and Marilyn at Las Hadas, Manzanillo, Mexico (1983)
  

 
 
Dick Creates Baxter Corporate Leasing Group
  
   
Dick returned to Baxter Corporate Headquarters in 1983 to create an in-house leasing division for Baxter. He directed this new entity and remained the guru of leasing in Baxter for the duration of his career. His efforts generated millions of dollars for Baxter in added earnings. In 1984, the family joined the First Presbyterian Church of Lake Forest.
 
 
Dick and family, Lake Forest, IL (1985)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn at Choir Costume Party
  
   
Dick joined the choir at the Presbyterian Church even before joining the church. The choir at this church has become like an extended family to us - sharing and caring together. Here Dick and Marilyn are attending a Halloween costume party at the home of Ron Larson with Marilyn disguised as a mermaid and Dick nattily dressed as Attila the Hun.
 
 
Marilyn and Dick at choir costume party, Lake Forest, IL (1986)
  

 
 
Dick with Leo, the Little Lion Dog
  
   
Chris pressed for the family to get a dog to replace Cassie. He saw a picture of a little lion dog (löwchen) and talked his mother into it, since she felt that he would never find one. The breed is one of rarest in the world. Chris, however, found a puppy in Franklin Park, IL. We named our little dog Leo. He was with us for 10 years and sadly, died while we were vacationing in Cancun, Mexico. Here Leo is babysitting Dick who is taking a short nap.
  
 
Dick with Leo, Lake Bluff, IL (1986)
  

 
 
Dick Sings at Curling Bonspiel
  
   
Dick was in high demand as an entertainer for curling bonspiels. In this Women's Bonspiel at the Chicago Curling Club, Dick came disguised as the father of William "the Fridge" Perry and sang "Old Man River" for the crowd. With Dick are four of our good curling friends from Canada.
  
 
Dick (as father of "the Fridge"), Northbrook, IL (1987)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn on Family European Trip
  
   
Marilyn's father died in 1984, and Marilyn used some of her inheritance to finance a family trip to Europe. Dick took a one-month vacation from Baxter and the family visited Paris, Monaco, Florence, Venice, Lucerne, Munich, Amsterdam, London, Stratford and Liverpool. Here we are in St. Mark's Square in Venice where Bill celebrated his 18th birthday.
  
 
Marilyn, Dick and Bill in St. Mark's Square, Venice, Italy (1987)
  

 
 
Dick Joins Camerata Singers
  
   
Dick joined the Camerata Singers as a singer in June 1985. That fall he joined the Board of the Camerata Society. In 1986, he was a co-founder of the Camerata Singers of Lake Forest, the group that superseded the Camerata Society. Dick has been a Board member for 21 years, serving as President for 6 years and as Treasurer for all the rest. He hasn't missed a single concert (89 in a row) since becoming a singer.
  
 
Dick - Camerata Board President, Lake Bluff, IL (1988)
  

 
 
Dick Celebrates 25 years at Baxter
  
   
In July, 1991, Baxter honored Dick for his 25 years with Baxter. They held a party and all three sons were able to attend. Dick was awarded a Baxter watch. Ironically, Dick had never earlier received any anniversary gifts from Baxter. He was always in the wrong place at the wrong time. You can see that all three sons are anxious to get Dick's 41-jewel Ginsbo-matic Swiss watch that he bought for $25 in 1962 while in the army.
  
 
Mike, Chris, Bill and Dick at Baxter 25th party, Deerfield, IL (1991)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn at Mike and Autumn's Wedding
  
   
A significant event for our family occurred when our oldest son, Mike, married Autumn Odell in Wichita, KS in 1991. Dick's mother, Julia, attended the wedding. Julia attended the college graduations of all twelve of her grand-children and the weddings of ten of them, but died in 1995 at age 86 prior to the marriage of Bill and Nancy.
  
 
Family photo at Mike's wedding, Wichita, KS (1991)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn attend John Paxson Day at Mercantile Exchange
  
   
Jack Sandner, President of the Mercantile Exchange and a member of the Camerata Board, asked Marilyn to persuade John Paxson, a neighbor of hers, to allow the Mercantile Exchange to sponsor a "John Paxson Day" to honor John for his championship winning shot for the Chicago Bulls. Marilyn was successful and we were invited to join John and Carolyn for an exciting day at the Mercantile Exchange. This picture is in Jack's office.
  
 
Dick, John and Carolyn Paxson and Jack Sandner at Mercantile Exchange, Chicago, IL (1992)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to Latvia
  
   
After graduating from college, Bill decided to spend 6 months teaching in a country recently freed from Communism. He selected Latvia and he was assigned to Salacgriva - a small town on the Baltic Sea, about 60 miles north of Riga. Dick and Marilyn visited him there and had a wonderful and mind-opening stay with his Latvian hosts. The trip included a guided tour of Latvia and stays in Riga, St. Petersburg, Russia and Tallinn, Estonia.
  
 
Dick and Bill visiting a cave in Latvia (1992)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn at Lake Forest Symphony Benefit
  
   
Marilyn was also very active in Camerata for 20 years, being the "sparkle" that helped Camerata succeed. Marilyn served as Camerata President for even more years than Dick. She was also active in the Women's Guild for the Lake Forest Symphony. At this benefit in 1993, Marilyn introduced the concept of donated French Market baskets which would be bid for in a silent auction. This concept raised more than $10,000 for the Symphony Guild.
  
 
Dick and Marilyn at Lake Forest Symphony Benefit, Lake Forest, IL (1993)
  

 
 
Dick and Leasing Team at Baxter
  
   
Life at Baxter wasn't always hard work. Each Halloween, most of the departments would select a theme and compete against each other in a fit of creativity and chutzpah. Leasing Services won the grand prize in 1995 with its entry focused on the TV show MASH. That's Dick with the little white hat in the second row!
  
 
Baxter Leasing Services team, Deerfield, IL (1995)
  

 
 
Dick and his Beer Cans
  
   
Dick is an avid collector of beer cans (all emptied from the bottom, of course) and has over 1500 different beer cans in his collection. This rare specimen, a king-size Budweiser can, is from the Czech Republic. This Czech company is quite old and easily predates the U.S. version (owned by Anheiser Busch). This feisty Czech company steadfastly refuses to sell out to the Busch family and has rights to the Budweiser name throughout Europe. Rev. Kinney (behind Dick) is part of this choir tour and he's making sure that Dick doesn't drink all of this beer while on the bus!
  
 
Dick with Czech Budweiser can, Czech Republic (1996)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn at Bill and Nancy's Wedding
  
   
Bill married Nancy Hrovatin in 1996 in an outdoor wedding at Discovery Park in Seattle, WA. Dick and his brothers sang "Sunrise, Sunset" from Fiddler on the Roof and were amazed to discover that a talented native Irish fiddler, Martin Hayes, the principal musician for the wedding, had never heard of the song!! They had to sing it 'a capella'.
 
 
Family photo at Bill's wedding, Seattle, WA (1996)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to Chile
  
   
Shortly after their wedding day, Bill and Nancy set off on a nearly 2-year long adventurous honeymoon, bicycling 12,000 km from Antarctica to Central American. We visited them in Patagonia. In addition to this boat trip to the glacier at Laguna San Rafael in southern Chile, we visited Santiago and Puerto Montt, and stayed at the luxurious Puyuhuapi thermal spa and at San Carlos de Bariloche in Argentina.
  
 
Dick and Marilyn at San Rafael glacier, Chile (1992)
  

 
 
Dick's Favorite Hobbies
  
   
No review of Dick would be complete without mentioning his two major hobbies, genealogy and puzzle-solving. He has managed to link every Grubaugh in the U.S. to Michael Grubaugh (b.1751), the grand patriarch of Marilyn's family. He has been a consistent winner in the grand-daddy of all puzzle contests - the annual Puzzle-Up, a masochistic and intense month-long brain challenge.
  
 
Dick does a crossword puzzle while babysitting Lauren, San Ramon, CA (1997)
  

 
 
Dick as Church Soloist
  
   
Dick has been a steady fixture in church choirs all of his life - initially as a paid soloist, but, now, for many years, as merely a dedicated volunteer. Marilyn and Dick have participated in five different choir tours - England/Wales; the Maritime Provinces in Canada; the Czech Republic/Austria/Hungary; Poland/Germany; and Scotland. A frequent soloist, here Dick is waiting to perform a solo at Lauren's christening.
 
 
Dick sings at Lauren's christening, Lake Forest, IL (1997)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn as Curlers
  
   
While in Canada, Dick and Marilyn learned the sport of curling - a very popular winter sport in Canada. When Dick relocated back to Illinois in 1983, he joined the Chicago Curling Club in Northbrook. This photo was taken during a bonspiel at the Nutmeg Curling Club in Connecticut showing Dick and Marilyn at work sweeping with corn brooms to get the stone into the "house".
  
 
Dick and Marilyn curling, Darien, CT (1998)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to Colombia
  
   
In 1998, Dick and Marilyn met up with Bill and Nancy again in South America, this time in Cartagena, Colombia. After failing to convince Bill to bypass Colombia by flying from Ecuador to Panama, Dick and Marilyn ironically joined them there. Dick and Bill are holding 3-toed sloths. This extremely phlegmatic animal looks at you intently as if asking "Now, again, what was the question?"
  
 
Dick and Bill with 3-toed sloths in Cartagena, Colombia (1998)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to Ireland
  
   
Dick and Marilyn spent two exciting weeks in Ireland in September 1999. Highlights include Dublin, Newgrange (ancient ruins north of Dublin), two nights at Ashford Castle, the cliffs of Moher, the Burren, the Ring of Kerry, Killarney, Kinsale, and, of course, Waterford. Most hectic was a cross-country car trip on small narrow country roads during a dark rainy night!! Each oncoming bus was quite an experience!! Ouch!!
  
 
Dick and Marilyn at Muckross House, Killarney, Ireland (1999)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn Meet the Smashing Pumpkins
  
   
Dick and Marilyn follow Chris's music career with much vicarious pleasure. When Chris was selected by Billy Corgan to join the Smashing Pumpkins on their final European tour, Dick and Marilyn decided to go to Rome and Milan to observe up close the life of rock stars. All of the Pumpkins were our guests at a restaurant in Milan for an interesting evening meal. While in Milan, we also saw Prokofiev's "War and Peace" at La Scala, performed by the Bolshoi Opera.
 
 
Dick boarding the Pumpkins' luxury bus in Milan, Italy (2000)
  

 
 
Dick Sings for Wedding in Canada
  
   
After Dick left Baxter, he created his own management consulting company, working for both Baxter and Allegiance on leasing issues. Trillium Health Centre in Mississauga, ON hired Dick to implement a partnership program between Trillium and selected major health care companies. While in Canada, Dick sang at an this outdoor wedding for the daughter of close friends in Mississauga.
 
 
Dick singing at Kathy Stewart's wedding, Mississauga, ON (2001)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to Alaska
  
   
After several years as a consultant, Dick finally retired, and he and Marilyn had more time to travel. Here they are shown boarding the Pacific Princess (the Love Boat) for a 2-week trip to Alaska. It was one of the best trips of their life, and included a helicopter ride to Mendenhall Glacier (near Juneau, AK) where the helicopter actually landed atop the glacier.
  
 
Dick and Marilyn on the "Love Boat", Alaska (2001)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn attend Holmes Family Reunion
  
   
Much of our travel now is directed toward visits with one or more of the children/grandchildren. This particular occasion was the Holmes family reunion in Estes Park, CO where all of Dick's siblings and their families got together to recognize the wonderful life of Dick's parents. While there, we visited the Stanley Hotel - the location for the Jack Nicholson horror movie, "The Shining".
 
 
Holmes Family Reunion, Estes Park, CO (2002)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn's Three Sons
  
   
All three sons graduated from college; Mike from Denver University, Bill from Trinity University (in San Antonio, TX), and Chris from the University of Chicago. Mike also obtained an MBA from American University in Washington, DC. Bill and Chris have both been ordained (on-line) and they both now have legally officiated marriages for friends.
 
 
Family photo, Colorado (2002)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn's Grandchildren
  
   
The family tree continues to grow. Dick is shown here with four of his grandchildren, in order from the left - Mac, Charlie, Olivia and Lauren. Lauren, the oldest, is the daughter of Mike and Autumn. The other three belong to Bill and Nancy. Ruby Marie was born to Bill and Nancy in March 2006 bringing the running tally to five.
 
 
Dick with his grandchildren, Seattle, WA (2003)
  

 
 
Dick and Marilyn go to the Big Island of Hawaii
  
   
We replaced a trip to China with a wonderful trip to Hawaii, the big island, and, later, a nostalgic trip to Bar Harbor, ME where Marilyn had gone to French camp as a teenager. Mike had been promoted to VP/China by Ciena and Autumn and Lauren had joined him in Beijing, China. Dick on the Big Island of Hawaii (2004)
  

 
 
Dick as a Chef
  
   
As a chef, Dick offers you a very limited menu, specializing only in boiled lobster and/or grilled cheese sandwiches. You can take your pick. Here he is with one of his specialties at a typical holiday dinner. His limited offering reminds us of a Georgian restaurant we visited in St. Petersburg, Russia. Although the printed menu had at least 50 selections, every time we would pick one of the selections, the waitress would repeatedly, but politely suggest just two of the items. We finally figured it out - they were the only two items that were actually available!!
  
 
Dick with his specialty, boiled lobster, Lake Bluff, IL (2004)
  

 
 
Dick Hosts Marilyn's Camerata Retirement Party
  
   
After a number of short-lived retirements from Camerata, Marilyn finally retired for good in 2005. Dick orchestrated a wonderful retirement party for her at Exmoor Country Club in Highland Park, IL. They are pictured here in front of an old photo of Exmoor.
  
 
Dick and Marilyn at Marilyn's Camerata retirement party, Highland Park, IL (2005)
  

 
 
Dick's Beach Boys
  
   
Dick's oldest son, Mike, recently attained his 40th birthday and the entire family joined him at Bill's home in Encinitas, CA to help him celebrate. Here Dick poses with his own "Beach Boys" near the ocean beach in Encinitas.
  
 
Bill, Dick, Chris and Mike at the beach, Encinitas, CA (2006)
  

 
 
What's Ahead - the Dreamers!
  
   
OK, you ask, what's next? You can see Dick looking out to the far horizon with Bill and Mike during that same trip to celebrate Mike's birthday. You can almost hear them thinking - "I don't know what's out there, but I bet it's going to be interesting, challenging and fun!!"
  
 
Dick, Bill and Michael gazing into the horizon, Encinitas, CA (2006)
